This is the question I asked myself before committing to building a company focused on investing in capital markets.

Today, investing is easier than ever. Platforms like [Sharesies](https://www.sharesies.nz/) have democratized access to stocks, allowing retail investors to participate with low fees, seamless custody, and even automated tax reporting. For most individuals, this is an incredible leap forward compared to the past.

In many ways, these platforms are *good enough*—and for a lot of people, they are the right choice.

But as I looked deeper, one question kept coming back:

*Is this the most efficient way to compound wealth over the long term?*

### **The Tax Reality Most Investors Overlook**

For individual investors, income from dividends is taxed at their personal income tax rate. In New Zealand, that
means high-income earners could be paying up to 35%.

Compare that with a company structure, where the tax rate is a flat 28%.

At first glance, a 5% difference may not seem meaningful. But investing is not about snapshots—it’s about
compounding.
